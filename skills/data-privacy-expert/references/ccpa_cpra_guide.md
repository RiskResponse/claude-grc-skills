# CCPA/CPRA Comprehensive Guide

## Overview

The California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), is California's comprehensive privacy law that grants California residents significant rights over their personal information.

**Effective Dates:**
- CCPA: January 1, 2020
- CPRA Amendments: January 1, 2023
- 2026 Regulations: January 1, 2026

**Official Citation**: California Civil Code §§ 1798.100-1798.199

**Enforcement Authority**: California Privacy Protection Agency (CPPA)

## Scope and Applicability

### Who Must Comply

Businesses that:
1. Collect personal information of California consumers
2. Do business in California
3. Meet one or more thresholds:
   - Annual gross revenues exceed $25 million
   - Buy, sell, or share personal information of 100,000+ consumers or households
   - Derive 50% or more of annual revenues from selling or sharing personal information

**"Business"** includes for-profit entities that:
- Determine purposes and means of processing
- Are organized or operated for profit
- Collect consumers' personal information

### Territorial Scope

Applies to California residents, including:
- Individuals physically in California
- Those domiciled in California
- California citizens temporarily outside state

### Exemptions

**Complete Exemptions:**
- Government agencies
- Nonprofit organizations (unless processing on behalf of business)

**Partial Exemptions (Temporary or Limited):**
- Employment data (limited exemption)
- Business-to-business (B2B) communications (limited exemption)
- Public information lawfully made available by government
- Consumer information covered by sector-specific laws (HIPAA, GLBA, FCRA, DPPA)
- Medical information covered by California medical privacy laws
- Clinical trial data

## Key Definitions

### Personal Information

Information that identifies, relates to, describes, is reasonably capable of being associated with, or could reasonably be linked, directly or indirectly, with a particular consumer or household.

**Categories Include:**
1. **Identifiers**: Name, alias, postal address, IP address, email, account name, SSN, driver's license, passport, etc.
2. **Customer Records**: Name, signature, address, telephone, employment, medical information, etc.
3. **Protected Classification Characteristics**: Age, race, gender, veteran status, etc.
4. **Commercial Information**: Purchase records, purchasing tendencies
5. **Biometric Information**: Physiological, biological, or behavioral characteristics
6. **Internet Activity**: Browsing history, search history, interaction with websites/apps
7. **Geolocation Data**: Physical location or movements
8. **Audio, Electronic, Visual Information**: Audio, electronic, visual, thermal, olfactory, or similar information
9. **Professional or Employment Information**: Job history, performance evaluations
10. **Education Information**: Records maintained by educational institution
11. **Inferences**: Profile reflecting preferences, characteristics, behavior, attitudes

### Sensitive Personal Information

Subset of personal information that includes:
1. Social security, driver's license, state ID, passport number
2. Account log-in, financial account, debit/credit card + security/access code
3. Precise geolocation
4. Racial or ethnic origin, religious or philosophical beliefs, union membership
5. Contents of mail, email, text messages (unless business is intended recipient)
6. Genetic data
7. Biometric information for identification
8. Health information
9. Sex life or sexual orientation information
10. **Neural data** (added in 2026 regulations)

**Neural Data**: Information generated by measuring activity of consumer's central or peripheral nervous system, used or intended to be used to infer mental or emotional state.

### Sale of Personal Information

Selling, renting, releasing, disclosing, disseminating, making available, transferring, or otherwise communicating orally, in writing, or by electronic means, a consumer's personal information to a third party for monetary or other valuable consideration.

**Exceptions:**
- Disclosures to service providers or contractors
- Disclosures to which consumer has consented
- Intentional interactions with third party
- Legally required disclosures

### Share/Sharing

Sharing, renting, releasing, disclosing, disseminating, making available, transferring, or otherwise communicating orally, in writing, or by electronic means, a consumer's personal information to a third party for cross-context behavioral advertising, whether or not for monetary or other valuable consideration.

### Service Provider

Entity that processes personal information on behalf of business for business purpose pursuant to written contract.

**Contract Must Prohibit:**
- Retaining, using, or disclosing personal information for any purpose other than performing services
- Selling or sharing personal information
- Retaining, using, or disclosing information outside direct business relationship

**Certification Required:**
Service provider must certify it understands restrictions and will comply.

### Contractor

Similar to service provider but for specific limited purposes:
- Detecting security incidents
- Resisting malicious, deceptive, fraudulent, or illegal actions
- Debugging to identify and repair errors
- Short-term, transient use (if not disclosed to third party)

## Consumer Rights

### Right to Know (§1798.100, §1798.110, §1798.115)

Consumers can request:

**Right to Know What Information Is Collected:**
- Categories of personal information collected
- Categories of sources
- Business or commercial purpose for collection
- Categories of third parties with whom information is shared

**Right to Know Specific Pieces:**
- Specific pieces of personal information collected

**Right to Know About Sales/Sharing:**
- Categories of personal information sold or shared
- Categories of third parties to whom sold or shared
- Categories of personal information disclosed for business purpose

**Response Timeline**: 45 days (extendable by 45 days once with notice)

**Format**: Deliver in readily usable format, free of charge

**Frequency Limit**: May limit to 2 verifiable requests per 12-month period

### Right to Delete (§1798.105)

Consumers can request deletion of personal information collected from them.

**Business Must:**
- Delete from records
- Direct service providers and contractors to delete

**Exceptions (Not Required to Delete If Necessary To):**
1. Complete transaction, provide goods/services, fulfill contract
2. Detect security incidents, protect against illegal activity
3. Debug to identify and repair errors
4. Exercise free speech or another right
5. Comply with California Electronic Communications Privacy Act
6. Engage in research in public interest with appropriate safeguards
7. Enable solely internal uses reasonably aligned with consumer expectations
8. Comply with legal obligation
9. Make internal lawful uses compatible with context provided

**Response Timeline**: 45 days (extendable by 45 days once)

### Right to Correct (§1798.106)

Consumers can request correction of inaccurate personal information.

**Business Must:**
- Use commercially reasonable efforts to correct
- Notify service providers and contractors to correct
- Correct in own records

**Response Timeline**: 45 days (extendable by 45 days once)

### Right to Opt-Out of Sale/Sharing (§1798.120)

Consumers can opt out of sale or sharing of personal information.

**Business Must:**
- Provide clear "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" link on homepage
- Respect Global Privacy Control (GPC) or similar opt-out preference signals
- Wait at least 12 months before asking opt-out consumer to opt back in

**No Required Account**: Opt-out must be available without creating account

**Third Party Collection**: If collecting from third party, honor prior opt-out if have actual knowledge

### Right to Limit Use of Sensitive Personal Information (§1798.121)

Consumers can limit use and disclosure of sensitive personal information.

**Business Must:**
- Provide "Limit the Use of My Sensitive Personal Information" link (if applicable)
- Limit use to:
  - Performing services reasonably expected
  - Certain enumerated purposes (security, quality, etc.)
- Respect opt-out preference signals

**Exceptions:**
- If only collection/use is for enumerated purposes, notice not required but must disclose

### Right to Non-Discrimination (§1798.125)

Businesses cannot discriminate against consumers for exercising CCPA rights by:
- Denying goods or services
- Charging different prices or rates
- Providing different level or quality of goods or services
- Suggesting consumer will receive different price or quality

**Financial Incentive Programs Permitted:**
- May offer financial incentives for collection, sale, or deletion
- Must be reasonably related to value of data
- Must provide notice and obtain opt-in consent
- Consumer can revoke consent anytime

## Business Obligations

### Notice at Collection (§1798.100(b))

**Required at or before collection**, must inform consumers of:
- Categories of personal information to be collected
- Purposes for which categories will be used
- Whether information is sold or shared
- Retention period or criteria for determining period
- Right to limit use of sensitive personal information (if applicable)

**Update**: Must update notice at least once every 12 months

### Privacy Policy (§1798.130(a)(5))

**Must Include:**
1. Categories of personal information collected in preceding 12 months
2. Categories of sources
3. Business or commercial purposes for collection
4. Categories of personal information sold or shared (if any)
5. Categories of personal information disclosed for business purpose (if any)
6. Categories of third parties to whom disclosed
7. Consumer rights (know, delete, correct, opt-out, limit)
8. How to submit requests
9. Process for verifying requests
10. Retention periods or criteria
11. Contact information for privacy inquiries

**Update**: At least once every 12 months

**Accessibility**: Post conspicuously on website

### Verifiable Consumer Requests

**Must Establish Process:**
- For submitting requests
- At least two methods (toll-free number and website)
- For businesses operating only online and with direct relationship, email acceptable

**Verification Requirements:**
- Match data points provided by consumer with information business already maintains
- More stringent for sensitive requests (deletion, specific pieces)
- Use reasonable security measures

**Cannot Require Account**: Must allow requests without creating account

**Response Timeline**: 45 days (extendable by 45 days)

**Denial**: Must explain reason and provide information about consumer's right to appeal

### Opt-Out Methods

**"Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" Link:**
- On homepage of website
- Clear and conspicuous
- Direct to opt-out or form

**Respect Opt-Out Preference Signals:**
- Global Privacy Control (GPC)
- Similar universal opt-out mechanisms
- Treat as valid opt-out request

**No Opt-In Required**: For consumers under 16, must obtain opt-in consent before selling/sharing

### Service Provider/Contractor Contracts

**Written Contract Must:**
- Specify that personal information is disclosed only for limited business purpose
- Prohibit retention, use, or disclosure outside performing services
- Prohibit selling or sharing personal information
- Grant business right to monitor compliance
- Require service provider/contractor to notify of inability to comply
- Grant right to take remedial action

**Service Provider Certification:**
Must certify understanding of restrictions and compliance commitment.

## 2026 CCPA Regulations (Effective January 1, 2026)

The California Privacy Protection Agency adopted new regulations on September 22, 2025, effective January 1, 2026.

### Cybersecurity Audits (§7400-7405)

**Who Must Comply:**
Businesses that process personal information and meet criteria:
- Process personal information of 1 million+ consumers annually
- Derive 50%+ revenue from sale/sharing of personal information

**Requirements:**
- Conduct annual cybersecurity audit
- Audit must assess and document cybersecurity practices
- Evaluate compliance with reasonable security procedures
- Performed by independent qualified assessor
- Submit certification of completion to CPPA

**Timeline:**
First cybersecurity audit reports due starting April 1, 2028, with staggered deadlines based on revenue:
- Businesses with $1 billion+ annual revenue: April 1, 2028
- Businesses with $500 million - $1 billion: April 1, 2029
- Businesses with $100 million - $500 million: April 1, 2030
- All other covered businesses: April 1, 2031

**Audit Must Address:**
- Governance and risk management
- Asset management and access controls
- Business environment and risk assessment
- Cybersecurity policies and procedures
- Detection and response capabilities
- Third-party risk management
- Training and awareness

**Documentation:**
- Maintain audit reports for 3 years
- Make available to CPPA upon request

### Risk Assessments (§7500-7505)

**When Required:**
Before processing activities that present significant risk to consumer privacy or security.

**Significant Risk Indicators:**
- Processing sensitive personal information
- Large-scale processing
- Sale or sharing of personal information
- Processing personal information of children
- Automated decision-making
- Profiling that could lead to foreseeable risk of harm
- Processing that involves innovative technology

**Assessment Must Include:**
1. **Identification of processing**: Description of processing operations, categories of data, purposes
2. **Assessment of benefits**: Benefits to business, consumers, other stakeholders
3. **Assessment of risks**: Potential adverse consequences to consumer privacy and security
4. **Evaluation of safeguards**: Technical and organizational measures to mitigate risks
5. **Balancing test**: Whether benefits justify risks, considering safeguards

**Timeline:**
- Risk assessments must begin by January 1, 2026
- Complete assessments for existing high-risk processing by April 1, 2028
- Update every 3 years or when material changes to processing
- Submit to CPPA by April 1, 2028 (first batch)

**Documentation:**
- Maintain for 3 years after superseded
- Submit to CPPA upon request or as required

### Automated Decision-Making Technology (ADMT) (§7600-7605)

**Effective**: January 1, 2027

**Definition:**
Technology used to make or facilitate decisions that produce legal or similarly significant effects concerning consumers.

**Requirements for Businesses Using ADMT:**

**Pre-Use Notice:**
Before using ADMT, provide notice that includes:
- That ADMT will be used
- Purpose of ADMT
- General description of how it works
- Categories of personal information processed
- Categories of third parties accessing personal information
- How consumer can opt out

**Opt-Out Right:**
Consumers have right to opt out of use of ADMT concerning them.

**Opt-Out Method:**
- Provide clear mechanism
- Include link or button: "Limit Use of ADMT" or similar
- Process opt-out within 15 business days

**Exceptions:**
- Fraud prevention and security
- Quality control
- Solely internal uses

**Access to Logic:**
Consumer can request:
- Meaningful information about logic involved in decision-making
- Ability to appeal decision
- Opportunity to obtain human review

### Updated Definition of Sensitive Personal Information

**Neural Data Added:**
Information generated by measuring activity of consumer's central or peripheral nervous system, when used or intended to be used to infer mental or emotional state.

**Examples:**
- EEG (electroencephalography) data
- Brain-computer interface data
- Peripheral nerve activity measurements
- Data from neural implants or sensors

**Implications:**
- Neural data subject to enhanced protections
- Consumers can limit use and disclosure
- Businesses must provide "Limit" notice if collected

### Insurance Regulations Clarification (§7300-7305)

**When Insurance Companies Must Comply:**
- When acting as business (not just providing insurance)
- When processing personal information beyond underwriting/claims
- When selling or sharing personal information

**Limited Compliance:**
For traditional insurance functions, limited CCPA obligations apply.

## Enforcement and Penalties

### California Privacy Protection Agency (CPPA)

Dedicated enforcement authority established by CPRA.

**Powers:**
- Adopt regulations
- Conduct investigations
- Issue subpoenas
- Hold hearings
- Order remedies
- Impose administrative fines

### Administrative Fines

**Violations:**
- Up to $2,500 per violation
- Up to $7,500 per intentional violation or violation involving children under 16

**Calculation:**
Each consumer affected can be separate violation.

### Private Right of Action (§1798.150)

**Limited to Data Breaches:**
Only for certain data breaches resulting from failure to maintain reasonable security.

**Requirements:**
- 30-day notice to business to cure
- If not cured, can sue for statutory damages

**Damages:**
- $100-$750 per consumer per incident, or
- Actual damages, whichever is greater
- Injunctive or declaratory relief
- Other relief court deems proper

**Standing:**
Consumer must show business's failure to maintain reasonable security was material contributing factor to breach.

### CPPA Enforcement Actions

Before administrative fine, CPPA must provide:
- 30-day notice
- Opportunity to cure (for first violations, if cure possible)

If violation not cured:
- CPPA may impose fines
- May order other relief

## Comparison: CCPA vs GDPR

### Similarities

- Consumer/data subject rights (access, deletion, portability)
- Requirements for privacy notices
- Restrictions on discrimination/retaliation
- Data security requirements
- Rights for children

### Key Differences

**Scope:**
- CCPA: California residents
- GDPR: EU/EEA residents

**Applicability:**
- CCPA: Businesses meeting revenue/volume thresholds
- GDPR: Any organization processing EU personal data

**Legal Bases:**
- CCPA: No formal "legal basis" requirement, but restrictions on use
- GDPR: Must establish legal basis before processing

**Consent:**
- CCPA: Required for selling data of minors under 16
- GDPR: One of six legal bases, subject to strict requirements

**Opt-In vs Opt-Out:**
- CCPA: Opt-out for sales/sharing (opt-in for minors)
- GDPR: Depends on legal basis; consent requires opt-in

**Definition of Personal Information:**
- CCPA: Includes household data
- GDPR: Only individual data

**Sensitive Data:**
- CCPA: Right to limit use, specific categories
- GDPR: Prohibited unless specific condition met

**Response Timelines:**
- CCPA: 45 days
- GDPR: 30 days

**Private Right of Action:**
- CCPA: Only for data breaches
- GDPR: General right for any violation

**Fines:**
- CCPA: Up to $7,500 per violation
- GDPR: Up to €20M or 4% of global revenue

## Compliance Checklist

### Initial Assessment
- [ ] Determine if CCPA applies (California consumers + threshold)
- [ ] Identify all personal information collected
- [ ] Map data flows (collection, use, disclosure, retention)
- [ ] Classify sensitive personal information
- [ ] Identify sales or sharing of personal information
- [ ] Assess 2026 regulations applicability (audits, risk assessments, ADMT)

### Privacy Notice Requirements
- [ ] Create/update privacy policy with all required elements
- [ ] Provide notice at collection for all collection points
- [ ] Post "Do Not Sell or Share" link on homepage (if applicable)
- [ ] Post "Limit Use of Sensitive Personal Information" link (if applicable)
- [ ] Disclose retention periods or criteria
- [ ] Update notices at least annually

### Consumer Rights Implementation
- [ ] Establish at least 2 methods for submitting requests
- [ ] Create verification process for consumer requests
- [ ] Implement 45-day response timeline
- [ ] Develop process for each right (know, delete, correct, opt-out, limit)
- [ ] Train staff on handling requests
- [ ] Create tracking system for requests

### Opt-Out Mechanisms
- [ ] Implement "Do Not Sell or Share" process
- [ ] Respect Global Privacy Control (GPC)
- [ ] Implement "Limit Use of Sensitive PI" process (if applicable)
- [ ] Ensure no account required for opt-out
- [ ] Wait 12 months before re-requesting opt-in

### Service Providers and Contractors
- [ ] Inventory all service providers and contractors
- [ ] Execute compliant contracts
- [ ] Obtain service provider certifications
- [ ] Ensure contracts include required provisions
- [ ] Monitor compliance

### Data Security
- [ ] Implement reasonable security measures
- [ ] Conduct security assessments
- [ ] Train employees on security
- [ ] Implement breach response plan
- [ ] Maintain security documentation

### 2026 Compliance (if applicable)
- [ ] Determine if cybersecurity audit required
- [ ] Schedule and conduct annual audits (starting 2028)
- [ ] Submit audit certifications per timeline
- [ ] Conduct risk assessments for high-risk processing
- [ ] Update risk assessments every 3 years
- [ ] Submit risk assessments to CPPA by April 1, 2028
- [ ] Prepare for ADMT requirements (by January 1, 2027)
- [ ] Provide ADMT notices and opt-out mechanisms
- [ ] Update systems to handle neural data as sensitive PI

### Documentation
- [ ] Maintain records of processing activities
- [ ] Document consumer requests and responses
- [ ] Retain audit reports (3 years)
- [ ] Retain risk assessments (3 years after superseded)
- [ ] Document data retention and deletion practices
- [ ] Keep service provider contracts and certifications

### Ongoing Compliance
- [ ] Update privacy policy annually
- [ ] Review and update data maps
- [ ] Monitor CPPA guidance and regulations
- [ ] Conduct periodic compliance audits
- [ ] Update training materials
- [ ] Review vendor compliance
- [ ] Track regulatory enforcement actions
