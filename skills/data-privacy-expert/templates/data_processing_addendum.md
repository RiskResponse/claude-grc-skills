## 1. DEFINITIONS

1.1 **"Applicable Laws"** means all applicable data protection and privacy laws and regulations, including without limitation the GDPR, CCPA/CPRA, and any other jurisdiction-specific privacy laws applicable to the processing of Personal Data under this DPA.

1.2 **"CCPA"** means the California Consumer Privacy Act, as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), California Civil Code §§ 1798.100 et seq.

1.3 **"Controller"** or **"Business"** means the entity that determines the purposes and means of Processing Personal Data. For purposes of GDPR, [Controller Name] is the Controller. For purposes of CCPA, [Business Name] is the Business.

1.4 **"Data Subject"** or **"Consumer"** means an identified or identifiable natural person to whom Personal Data relates.

1.5 **"GDPR"** means Regulation (EU) 2016/679 of the European Parliament and of the Council on the protection of natural persons with regard to the processing of personal data.

1.6 **"Personal Data"** or **"Personal Information"** means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person as defined by Applicable Laws, including but not limited to information defined as "personal data" under GDPR and "personal information" under CCPA.

1.7 **"Personal Data Breach"** means a breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to Personal Data.

1.8 **"Processing"** means any operation or set of operations performed on Personal Data, including collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure, transmission, dissemination, alignment, combination, restriction, erasure, or destruction.

1.9 **"Processor"** or **"Service Provider"** means the entity that Processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller. For purposes of GDPR, [Processor Name] is the Processor. For purposes of CCPA, [Service Provider Name] is the Service Provider.

1.10 **"Sub-processor"** means any entity engaged by Processor to Process Personal Data on behalf of Controller.

1.11 **"Supervisory Authority"** means an independent public authority established by a Member State pursuant to GDPR Article 51.

1.12 Terms not defined in this DPA shall have the meanings given to them in the Agreement or Applicable Laws.

## 3. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

3.1 **Controller Responsibilities**

The Controller:
- (a) Shall ensure it has a lawful basis for Processing under Applicable Laws;
- (b) Shall ensure it has authority to transfer Personal Data to Processor;
- (c) Shall provide Processor with necessary instructions and information;
- (d) Shall be solely responsible for the accuracy, quality, and legality of Personal Data and the means by which it acquired Personal Data;
- (e) Shall ensure that Processor is provided with all necessary information to comply with Applicable Laws;
- (f) Shall respond to Data Subject requests, with assistance from Processor as set forth herein.

3.2 **Processor Responsibilities**

The Processor:
- (a) Shall Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from Controller;
- (b) Shall comply with all obligations set forth in this DPA;
- (c) Shall immediately inform Controller if, in Processor's opinion, an instruction infringes Applicable Laws;
- (d) Shall ensure that persons authorized to Process Personal Data are bound by appropriate confidentiality obligations;
- (e) Shall implement and maintain appropriate technical and organizational security measures;
- (f) Shall assist Controller with compliance obligations as detailed herein.

## 6. PROCESSOR OBLIGATIONS

### 6.1 Processing Instructions

Processor shall:
- (a) Process Personal Data only on documented written instructions from Controller, including with regard to international transfers, unless required to Process by EU or Member State law, in which case Processor shall inform Controller of such legal requirement prior to Processing (unless prohibited by law);
- (b) Not Process Personal Data for any purpose other than as instructed by Controller and as necessary to provide the services under the Agreement;
- (c) Immediately inform Controller if Processor becomes aware that it cannot comply with instructions or Applicable Laws.

### 6.2 Confidentiality

Processor shall:
- (a) Ensure that all persons authorized to Process Personal Data are subject to a binding duty of confidentiality (whether by contract or statutory obligation);
- (b) Provide appropriate training to personnel who Process Personal Data;
- (c) Limit access to Personal Data to those personnel who require access to perform their duties under the Agreement.

### 6.3 Security of Processing

Processor shall implement and maintain appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, as detailed in **Schedule B** (Security Measures), including:

- (a) Pseudonymization and encryption of Personal Data where appropriate;
- (b) Ability to ensure ongoing confidentiality, integrity, availability, and resilience of Processing systems and services;
- (c) Ability to restore availability and access to Personal Data in a timely manner in the event of a physical or technical incident;
- (d) Regular testing, assessment, and evaluation of the effectiveness of technical and organizational measures;
- (e) Measures to identify vulnerabilities with regard to Processing of Personal Data in systems used to provide services under the Agreement.

Security measures shall take into account:
- State of the art;
- Costs of implementation;
- Nature, scope, context, and purposes of Processing;
- Risk of varying likelihood and severity for the rights and freedoms of Data Subjects.

## 7. PERSONAL DATA BREACH NOTIFICATION

7.1 **Processor's Notification Obligation**

Processor shall notify Controller without undue delay, and in any event within [24] hours, after becoming aware of a Personal Data Breach.

7.2 **Notification Content**

The notification shall include, to the extent available:

- (a) Description of the nature of the Personal Data Breach, including where possible:
  - Categories and approximate number of Data Subjects affected;
  - Categories and approximate number of Personal Data records affected;
- (b) Name and contact details of Processor's point of contact for the breach;
- (c) Description of the likely consequences of the Personal Data Breach;
- (d) Description of measures taken or proposed by Processor to address the Personal Data Breach and to mitigate its possible adverse effects.

7.3 **Phased Notification**

If all information is not available within [24] hours, Processor shall provide information in phases without undue further delay as it becomes available.

7.4 **Notification Method**

Breach notifications shall be sent to:
[INSERT CONTROLLER CONTACT INFORMATION]

7.5 **Cooperation**

- (b) Provide reasonable assistance to Controller in meeting Controller's breach notification obligations under Applicable Laws;
- (c) Preserve evidence and maintain forensic records as reasonably directed by Controller;
- (d) Coordinate communications regarding the breach with Controller prior to any external notification.

## 11. LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION

11.1 **Liability Standard**

Each party's liability under this DPA shall be subject to the limitations of liability set forth in the Agreement, except that:

- (a) Neither party's liability shall be limited with respect to violations of Applicable Laws that result in fines, penalties, or damages assessed by governmental authorities or awarded to Data Subjects;
- (b) [Under GDPR, each party shall be liable only for damage caused by Processing that violates obligations specifically directed to that party under GDPR or where it has acted outside or contrary to lawful instructions].

11.2 **Allocation of Liability**

Where both Controller and Processor are involved in the same Processing and both are liable:

- (a) Each party shall be liable for the entire damage in order to ensure effective compensation of the Data Subject;
- (b) Where a party has paid full compensation, that party shall be entitled to claim back from the other party that part of the compensation corresponding to the other party's part of responsibility for the damage.

11.3 **Indemnification**

Processor shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Controller from and against any and all claims, liabilities, damages, losses, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from:

- (d) Unauthorized Sub-processor engagement;
